I couldn't remember the last rock show I attended. It was well before I became a parent in 2022, and some time before the Covid-19 pandemic. I was lucky to get out on a Monday evening (March 30) to see [Ratboys](https://www.ratboysband.com/) and [villagerrr](https://www.villagerrr.com/) at Launchpad here in Albuquerque.

The current tour is called "When the Sun Explodes" and the two groups are on the West Coast this week. Ratboys is touring in support of their new album <cite><a href="https://ratboys.bandcamp.com/album/singin-to-an-empty-chair">Singin' To An Empty Chair</a></cite> while villagerrr has an upcoming album called <cite><a href="https://villagerrr.bandcamp.com/album/carousel">Carousel</a></cite> out in May. 

Launchpad is a little venue on Central Ave (Route 66) in downtown ABQ. As the bands were working their own merch table, I managed to say hi to Dave Sagan and Julia Steiner from Ratboys and told them how much [I dig the new album](/notes/2026-02-02-ratboys-signin.html). Villagerrr had a bunch of 3D printed "ICE OUT" whistles at the table for free and Julia closed the Ratboys set with a brief speech about resistance and fighting apathy at this present moment in the U.S.

I was able to get a few photos on my phone. This post is about a week late as I was on vacation in Ghost Ranch and northern NM the morning after the show through Easter weekend.
