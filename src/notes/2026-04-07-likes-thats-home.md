<p>The photograph's official name, according to NASA, is <cite><a href="https://www.astronomy.com/space-exploration/artemis-2-snaps-a-blue-marble-for-our-times/">Hello, World</a></cite>. A programmer's joke, a first proof that the system is working. But also a greeting. A wave across an incomprehensible distance. We are still here. It is the first image of the complete Earth taken by human hands since <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Blue_Marble">December 7, 1972</a>, 54 years ago. For my entire lifetime, no human being had looked at Earth whole from out there. We had orbited, we had sent probes, we had assembled composites from satellite data. But nobody had pressed a shutter. Nobody had seen it the way you see a face.</p>
